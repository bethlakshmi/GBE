2014 Performers' Application

Thank you for your interest in applying to perform at The eighth annual Great Burlesque Exposition of 2014.

GBE8 is February 7-9, 2014 at The Hyatt Regency Hotel in Cambridge, MA.

Our form has changed since last year. Please read over everything carefully, and fill out the form to the best of your ability.

Incomplete applications will not be accepted, but you can now save your application and come back to complete it. Also, every time you log in to the site, you'll get a notice letting you know your application isn't complete. Applications are accepted from September 1 through October 31 and performers are notified of acceptance on or about December 1.

Unpaid applications will not be accepted but we'll send you a PayPal invoice so you can settle up before October 31st.

There are four potential performance slots for applicants on three nights:
  • Friday night, February 7 during The Burlesque Bordello. This isn't the same thing as The Rhinestone Review which is earlier in the evening on Friday and invitation only. Performances at The Burlesque Bordello Party are on a small (8'x8') stage, are non-competition, and are best suited for solos or duets. This is limited to 10-15 acts of varying lengths. We are also accepting NON-burlesque variety acts for this show
  • Saturday night, February 8 The Main Event, where you can perform either in competition or in exhibition (while the judges or off selecting the winners). The competition portion of The Main Event is limited to about 25 acts each act in competition has a maximum length of four minutes and fifteen seconds (04:15). Most people don't get in the first time they apply, so don't feel badly if you don't get in.
  • Sunday night, February 9 The Original Newcomer's Showcase We don't have a hard cut-off for what a 'newcomer' is. We use our judgment based on the biographical information you provide us. Someone who has been performing for only a year but has already been in three festivals and dances regularly in a weekly showcase is technically "newer" than someone who has been performing for three years, but only performs once or twice a year. We accept 10-15 performers for this show.
  • Sunday night, February 9 The Sideshow Ball is the other part of Sunday's entertainment. It's for freaks, geeks, and variety entertainers. In past years we've had belly dancers, snake charmers, magicians, mentalists, comedians, grinder girls, sword swallowers, hula hoopers, watchamacallems and thingamawhosits. We accept 8-10 variety acts and intersperse them amongst the burlesque newcomers.


At minimum, before applying, you'll want:
  • to know which act you're going to submit
  • a picture of yourself and a video (preferably of the act with which you are applying)
  • a little burlesque resume.
  • a PayPal account, checkbook, or credit card.

Application Hints

The selection committee wants you to perform, but they also want to create the best show possible. While some of the members of the committee may know you, most of them won't, so your application is the only information they have about your act and you as a performer. When filling out the form, try and imagine how it reads to someone who doesn't know you and has never seen your act. Remember that we get hundreds of applications; while we'd love to be able to follow-up and ask questions on every submission, odds are we won't be able to. If something isn't clear on your application, you're less likely to be accepted.

Submissions that have video go to the top of the pile. Submissions that have clear video of the act which is being submitted go to the top of the top of the pile. "Clear" video doesn't mean 'professional' video, it just means steady, well-let, and in focus.

The Great Burlesque Exposition does not have a preferred 'style' of act. We like good acts, but besides that, all flavors of performance are welcome.

Be careful not to paint yourself into a corner. If you've been performing for 5 years, but only want to be considered for The Original Newcomers' Showcase, or if your act is six minutes long, but you indicate you only want to be considered for The Main Event, you've made our decision for us — and taken yourself out of the running.
