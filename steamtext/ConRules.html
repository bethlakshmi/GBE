Rules

The Most Important Rule of All

Have a good time! Have a great time! Have a grand time! Just have fun!

Do Unto Others

Treat other convention attendees and hotel guests in a generally civilized fashion. Abusive, insulting, intimidating, bothersome, or outwardly illegal behavior will not be tolerated.

Communicate With Your GMs

If you know in advance that you cannot make an event that involved precasting characters, please let the GMs or Operations Staff know, so that the GMs can make proper arrangements. If you are more than 10 minutes late for a game, you may be recast/dropped at the event runners' discretion.

Convention Policies

Game Registration Policy

follows a strict first come, first serve policy when it comes to game registrations.

The only exception to this policy is if there is some legal restriction that should exclude a player from a game. For example, if a game is a live combat game, there may be insurance reasons that require restrictions, such as insurance waivers for pregnant women and players under the age of 16. In these cases, it will be clear from the game description.

Harassment Policy

All attendees at are expected to treat other attendees, guests, staff, and the general public with respect. Physical and verbal harassment and sexual assault will not be tolerated. Roleplaying can be an intense activity, and the con staff wishes to provide a safe environment for the con-goers. If you experience harassment or if someone is making you uncomfortable, please inform the Con Chair or Operations Staff so we can handle the problem.

Be Nice to the Site - Signs

DO NOT, in any way, place signage or attach anything to the walls, doors or any other part of the Queen Mary. The Queen Mary is a historic vessel and has strict and EXPENSIVE rules governing the placement of signs.

Should you need a sign, please contact >The Con Chair and an appropriate sign will be constructed and placed for you.
